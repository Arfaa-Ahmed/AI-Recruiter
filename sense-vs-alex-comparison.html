Key Insights

✓ Sense Strengths
Higher conversion: 48.1% overall vs Alex's 34.0%

Better connection rate: 62.4% connect vs Alex's 47.8% response

Strong post-engagement: 77.2% complete after connecting

Real-time engagement: Voice calls create immediate interaction
✓ Alex Strengths
Higher volume: 5,839 invitations vs Sense's 3,215 candidates

More completions: 1,986 total vs Sense's 1,547

Strong automation ROI: 4.8 interviews/hour throughput

Scalable outreach: SMS allows mass distribution
⚠ Sense Challenges
Pre-connection drop-off: 37.6% never connect (voicemail/no answer)

Lower total volume: Processed fewer candidates overall

Timing dependency: Performance varies significantly by time of day
⚠ Alex Challenges
Low initial response: 52.2% of invitations remain pending

Candidate activation: Biggest bottleneck is getting recipients to act

Channel limitations: SMS requires candidate-initiated action vs real-time engagement
Critical Context
These systems are NOT directly comparable. Sense uses proactive voice calls (phone rings, candidate answers) while Alex uses passive SMS invitations (candidate must initiate scheduling). Sense's higher conversion (48.1% vs 34.0%) reflects the advantage of real-time synchronous engagement vs asynchronous text-based activation. Direct comparison requires channel normalization — for example, comparing Sense's voice performance to Alex's performance if Alex also made outbound calls, or comparing SMS-to-SMS engagement rates.
