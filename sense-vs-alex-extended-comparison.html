Key Insights

Sense Strengths

  • Higher end-to-end conversion: 43.2% vs Alex's 36.4% — voice outreach drives an advantage in overall yield
  • Superior connection rate: 77.7% connect rate vs Alex's 51.1% response — real-time phone engagement dramatically outperforms passive SMS activation
  • Lower pre-engagement drop-off: Only 22.3% never connect vs 48.9% of Alex invites still pending — proactive dialing removes the "candidate must act first" bottleneck

Alex Strengths

  • Higher volume throughput: 13,320 invitations vs 11,172 candidates — SMS scales more easily for mass outreach
  • Strongest post-engagement completion: 71.1% of candidates who act ultimately complete, vs Sense's 55.5% — once engaged, Alex converts at a significantly higher rate
  • Quantifiable ROI: 1,148 recruiter hours saved (143 workdays) with 14.3 min saved per interview — clear, measurable operational impact

Sense Challenges

  • High in-call drop rate: 44.5% of connected candidates don't complete — 2,127 candidate hang-ups and 1,380 agent-initiated disconnects indicate friction during the call itself
  • Voicemail bottleneck: 4,908 calls (42.3% of no-contact) hit voicemail — significant volume reaching dead ends without a strong callback mechanism
  • Lower total volume processed: 11,172 candidates vs Alex's 13,320 invitations — voice outreach is inherently less scalable

Alex Challenges

  • Massive activation gap: 48.9% of invitations (6,510) remain in "Sent" status — nearly half of all outreach goes unactioned
  • Stall friction: 16.3% of engaged candidates stall (1,107 total) — paused and incomplete status suggests workflow or candidate experience issues
  • Channel limitation: SMS requires candidate-initiated action — unlike voice, there's no mechanism to create immediate real-time engagement

Critical Context

These systems are NOT directly comparable. Sense uses proactive voice calls (phone rings, candidate answers) while Alex uses passive SMS invitations (candidate must initiate scheduling). Sense's higher conversion (43.2% vs 36.4%) reflects the inherent advantage of real-time synchronous engagement vs asynchronous text-based activation. Direct comparison requires channel normalization — for example, comparing Sense's voice performance to Alex's if Alex also made outbound calls, or comparing SMS-to-SMS response rates.


Notably, over the extended 10-month period both platforms delivered nearly identical completed interview volumes (Sense: 4,821 vs Alex: 4,843), suggesting they serve complementary roles in the recruiting funnel: Sense excels at high-conversion targeted outreach while Alex provides scalable, automation-driven volume with measurable recruiter time savings.
