Sense AI - Biweekly Snapshot

Mar 1 – 15, 2026 · 15-Day Analysis

Executive Summary

This biweekly snapshot covers Sense AI's end-to-end performance from March 1–15, 2026, spanning both Top-of-Funnel (TOF) voice outreach activity and Bottom-of-Funnel (BOF) Salesforce pipeline outcomes.

Top of Funnel: Sense made 4,685 call attempts across 1,865 unique candidates. 67.9% connected (1,267) and 45.1% completed the interaction (842). Once connected, completion is strong at 66.5%. The platform averaged 2.51 attempts per candidate, with positive sentiment on 60.5% of connected calls.

Bottom of Funnel: The Sense integration moved 665 job submissions to "Reviewing" in Salesforce. 44.4% had further activity (295) while 55.6% remain idle (370, avg 16.4 days). Through the screening path, 295 job submissions progressed with 8.5% reaching handoff (25), 8.8% to client submission (26), 26.9% of submissions to interview requested (7), 71.4% of requested completed (5), 2 offers accepted, no confirmed placements this period and 2 placement falloffs recorded. Through the AI matching engine, 3,808 job submissions were matched across 90 jobs. Of those outreached, 185 expressed interest (4.9%), 10.8% of interested reached handoff (20), 95% of handoffs made it to client submission (19), and 5.3% of submissions had an interview requested (1). Offer and placement data are not yet visible for the AI matching cohort within this window.

Key findings: The primary TOF bottleneck is candidate drop-off after connection - 33.5% of connected candidates (425) do not complete the interaction, with early hang-ups within the first 60 seconds as the leading cause. On the BOF side, 370 job submissions screened by Sense are sitting idle in Reviewing, representing recruiter follow-through as a secondary bottleneck. The matching engine's high recruiter decline rate (287) signals a match quality issue that, if addressed, could significantly improve downstream conversion without increasing outreach volume.

Strong Connection & Conversion Profile
Finding: 67.9% connection rate and 45.1% end-to-end conversion are healthy for a 15-day window. Once connected, 66.5% complete, indicating the voice flow is performing well. Positive sentiment at 60.5% of connected calls reinforces that candidates are engaging substantively.
Early Candidate Drop-off Signal
Finding: 64.8% of in-call drops (524 calls, 406 candidates) are user hang-ups, with a median terminated call duration of 45 seconds. Candidates are disengaging very early, likely within the opening script. Reviewing the first 60 seconds of the voice flow is a high-priority optimization.
Blank Disconnection Reasons - Data Quality Drag
Finding: 1,069 no-contact calls (35.4% of drops, 1,008 unique candidates) have no disconnection reason recorded. This limits the ability to distinguish between dialer failures, carrier issues, and candidate-side problems.

Matching Insight: Sense's 3.4× higher outreach volume (3,808 vs Alex's 1,110) does not translate to proportionally better outcomes, it comes at a steep cost. Sense's 287 recruiter declines signal a significant "filler" problem: the majority of matches beyond the top tier are low quality, creating recruiter overhead and candidate fatigue from irrelevant outreach. Alex's tighter match settings (25 max vs Sense's 50) keep the pipeline lean and purposeful - fewer submissions, but less wasted effort on both the recruiter and candidate side. The higher interest rate on Sense (4.9% vs Alex's 1.4%) is largely a reflection of volume, not quality, and quality is what converts downstream.
