Alex AI - Biweekly Snapshot

Mar 1 – 15, 2026 · 15-Day Analysis

Executive Summary

This biweekly snapshot covers Alex AI's end-to-end performance from March 1–15, 2026, spanning both Top-of-Funnel (TOF) screening activity and Bottom-of-Funnel (BOF) Salesforce pipeline outcomes.

Top of Funnel: Grace sent 2,831 SMS invitations. 55.7% of candidates responded (1,578) and 36% completed their AI interview (1,009 completions). Once a candidate responds, completion is strong at 63.9%, indicating the interview experience itself is effective. The platform averaged 66 completed interviews per day, accumulating 439 hours of automated recruiter activity, equivalent to ~29 hours/day of human recruiter capacity.

Bottom of Funnel: The Apriora integration moved 973 candidates to "Reviewing" in Salesforce during this time period. 51.1% had further activity (497) while 48.9% remain idle (476, avg 14.9 days). Through the screening/application path, 470 job submissions progressed, with 12.1% reaching handoff (57), 11.7% to client submission (55), 20% of submissions to interview requested (11), 36.4% of requested completed (4), 3 offers accepted, and 5 placements. Through the AI matching engine, 1,110 job submissions were matched across 83 jobs, with a 90.8% auto-campaign rate (1,009 outreached). Of those outreached, 1.4% expressed interest (14), 64.3% of interested reached handoff (9), 66.7% of handoffs made it to client submission (6), and 16.7% of submissions had an interview requested (1).

Key findings: The primary TOF bottleneck is candidate response activation - 44.3% of invitations (1,253) remain in "Sent" status with no action. On the BOF side, 476 candidates screened by Grace are sitting idle in Reviewing, representing recruiter follow-through as a secondary bottleneck.

Solid Overall Conversion
Finding: Alex achieved a 36% overall interview completion rate (1,009 completed out of 2,831 invitations). Once candidates respond, 63.9% complete, indicating the interview experience itself is effective and the primary lever is getting more candidates to engage with the initial SMS.
Primary Bottleneck: Candidate Response Activation
Finding: 44.3% of invitations (1,253) remain in "Sent" status with no candidate action. This is the single largest funnel leak. Improving SMS messaging copy, send timing, or adding follow-up nudges could materially lift response rates and downstream completions.
Positive Candidate Experience Signal
Finding: Candidate feedback ratings skew strongly toward 4–5 stars (~90%+ of responses) with minimal 1–2 star detractors. This provides a supportive directional signal on interview quality and candidate experience with the AI recruiter.
Strong Automation ROI
Finding: 439 hours of automated interviewing in 15 days represents ~29 hours/day of recruiter-equivalent capacity. At 66 completed interviews/day, Alex is delivering throughput that would require multiple full-time human recruiters, demonstrating clear automation-driven productivity.

Matching Insight: Of 1,110 job submissions across 83 jobs, 1,009 were auto-campaigned (90.8%). Alex's tighter match settings (25 max vs Sense's 50) produce a leaner, higher-quality pipeline - 9 handoffs converted to 6 client submissions (66.7% handoff-to-submission rate vs Sense's 95%, but on a cleaner candidate set with far fewer recruiter declines). Sense's higher interest rate (4.9% vs 1.4%) comes at the cost of 287 recruiter declines, a volume-over-quality tradeoff that creates candidate fatigue and recruiter overhead. Alex's approach prioritizes precision: fewer but better-matched candidates, less wasted recruiter effort.
