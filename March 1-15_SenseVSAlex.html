This creates recruiter overhead and candidate fatigue from irrelevant outreach. Alex's tighter match limits keep the pipeline lean and purposeful.

Key Insights

Where Sense Leads

  • Broader reach: 4,039 unique candidates contacted vs 2,831 invitations. Sense's outreach casts a wider net
  • Matching volume: 3,808 matches vs 1,168 with 100% campaign rate. Higher outreach ceiling produces more interested candidates (185 vs 35)
  • Handoff → Submission: 100% of handoffs convert to client submissions vs 92.6%. Every accepted candidate moves forward
  • Interview completion: 75% of requested interviews are completed vs 30.8%. Stronger candidate follow-through at interview stage

Where Alex Leads

  • Placements: 5 placements vs 0 - the metric that directly drives revenue
  • Conversion efficiency: 36% end-to-end vs 20.9%. Alex produces higher per-candidate yield across all funnel stages
  • Pipeline throughput: 973 reviewed, 68 handoffs, 63 client submissions - all materially higher than Sense
  • Automation ROI: 440 hours saved in 15 days (~29 hrs/day equivalent), quantifiable recruiter capacity gain
  • Match quality: 40 recruiter declines vs 287. Less wasted recruiter effort, less candidate fatigue

Sense - Key Risks

  • Zero placements this period despite 2 offers accepted - downstream conversion is not materializing
  • Low connection rate: Only 36.4% of candidates are reached, with 63.6% (2,570) never connected. The primary bottleneck is reaching candidates
  • In-call friction: 42.5% of connected candidates don't complete, mostly hanging up within 60 seconds of the voice flow opening
  • Match quality signal: 287 recruiter declines = high "filler" volume creating overhead without proportional outcomes

Alex - Key Risks

  • Activation gap: 44.2% (1,250) of invitations never get a response, the single largest funnel leak
  • Search & Match underperformance: Only 6% conversion (54/981) vs 52% on Application path - outbound engagement is weak
  • Large paused pool: 480 candidates (17%) stalled, representing potentially recoverable volume
  • Interview completion rate: Only 30.8% of requested interviews complete (4 of 13), indicating candidate drop-off at a critical late stage

Bottom Line

Alex is outperforming Sense on the metrics that drive revenue this period: 5 placements vs 0, 63 client submissions vs 45, 68 handoffs vs 45, and 440 hours of quantifiable automation savings. Alex also holds a clear edge in conversion efficiency - 36% end-to-end vs Sense's 20.9% - with higher response rates (55.8% vs 36.4%) and completion rates (64.0% vs 57.5%) at every funnel stage.

Sense's primary advantage is reach: 4,039 candidates contacted with a wider matching engine (3,808 job subs across 90 jobs). Sense's connection rate is 36.4%, making its biggest bottleneck simply reaching candidates. Of those who do connect, 42.5% drop off early in the voice flow.

The matching engine comparison remains stark: Sense pushes 3.3× more volume but generates 7× more recruiter declines (287 vs 40) without proportionally more downstream outcomes. This creates recruiter overhead and candidate fatigue without delivering the conversion that justifies the volume.
